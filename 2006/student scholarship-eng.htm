TAAMN Scholarships

 Application Form

TAAMN has established two scholarships to provide financial assistance to students from Taiwan who are studying at the University of Minnesota-Twin Cities. Each scholarship provides a $1,500 cash award and a certificate. The applicant should submit one application only. TAAMN will select two recipients for the two scholarships.

 1. Li-Huey Lai Memorial Scholarship

Scholarship Description: The purpose of Li-Huey Lai Memorial Scholarship is to commemorate the life of Li-Huey Lai, a compassionate woman who made significant contribution to the Taiwanese American community in the Twin Cities. A native of Taipei, Taiwan, Li-Huey devoted her life to the promotion of Taiwan and its culture in Minnesota. In memory of her dedication, the scholarship provides need-based financial assistance to students from Taiwan who are studying at the University of Minnesota-Twin Cities. The scholarship carries a $1,500 cash award and a certificate.

 2. Suji Lee Scholarship

Scholarship Description: A native of Luodong, Taiwan, Suji Lee has lived in the Twin Cities for more than forty years. A successful business woman who has strong love of Taiwan, she established the scholarship to provide financial assistance to students from Taiwan who are studying at the University of Minnesota-Twin Cities. The scholarship carries a $1,500 cash award and a certificate.

Eligibility: The scholarships are open to both undergraduate and graduate students from Taiwan who currently are not receiving full financial aids, including fellowship, teaching and research assistantship, either from the Taiwan government or from the University of Minnesota-Twin Cities.  The applicant must be a full-time student registered at the University of Minnesota-Twin Cities and has not completed the required course/research work for graduation. The applicant must be a member of the Taiwan Student Association (TSA) at the University of Minnesota-Twin Cities. The awards are intended to help the recipients make timely progress toward graduation. Past recipients are not eligible to apply.

 Application: The applicant should submit one copy of the following documents: 1) Completed application form, 2) Official transcript, 3) Proof of registration for the coming academic year,

4) Two letters of endorsement, one from the TSA president indicating current TSA membership and the other from the faculty adviser certifying the applicant not receiving any fellowship, scholarship or assistantship from the department, and 5) One page personal statement in English. The statement should include information about the applicant’s background, financial need, long-term goals, achievements, and service to the TSA or Taiwanese community.

 The application form can be downloaded from the TAAMN website (www.taamn.org) or the TAAMN May Newsletter.

 Review and Selection: The scholarship committee is consisted of the following members: Tsan-Kuo Chang, Cheng-Cher Huang, Wei-Shou Hu, John Ko, Juey Hong Lai, Chi-Ming Tseng, Ming Wang and the president of TAAMN.  The review and selection will be based on the following criteria: financial need, overall academic record, merit of personal statement, service to the TSA or the Taiwanese community, and recommendation from the faculty adviser.  Preference will be given to the applicant who is making timely progress toward graduation.

 Deadline: The application must be post-marked on or before August 1.  The recipients will be announced on September 1 and posted on the TAAMN website and TSA website.  The awards will be presented at the annual Welcome Party of TAAMN held in September.  The recipients must be present to receive the scholarship.
